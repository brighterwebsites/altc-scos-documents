# **BRIGHTER WEBSITES - FEATURED IMAGE STYLE GUIDE**
## **NanoBanana / Image Generation Instructions**

---

## **Brand Visual Identity**

**Core Aesthetic:** Cinematic technical storytelling - professional but not corporate, technical but not sterile, strategic but approachable.

**Feeling:** Sophisticated infrastructure thinking, grounded expertise, regional authenticity with technical depth.

**NOT:** Generic tech stock photos, obvious website/computer imagery, bright studio shots, illustrated graphics.

---

## **Visual Style Framework**

### **Lighting & Mood**

**Primary Styles (Rotate Between):**

1. **Moody Cinematic** (40% of images)
   - Dark backgrounds with bokeh lights
   - Dramatic side lighting
   - Depth through blur and focus
   - Professional workspace aesthetic
   - Examples: Workspace with ambient lighting, nighttime cityscapes, evening office scenes

2. **Clean Industrial** (30% of images)
   - Soft natural light
   - White or minimal backgrounds
   - Architectural details
   - Precision product photography
   - Examples: Steel structures, concrete foundations, precision instruments

3. **Dramatic Natural** (20% of images)
   - Landscape/environmental metaphors
   - Golden hour or twilight lighting
   - Real-world grounding
   - Silhouettes and strong contrast
   - Examples: Regional landscapes, weather patterns, natural structures

4. **Motion/Energy** (10% of images)
   - Speed blur, light trails
   - Dynamic composition
   - High-tech aesthetic without being literal
   - Unexpected metaphors
   - Examples: Vehicles in motion, light patterns, kinetic energy

**Never Use:**
- Bright studio lighting on tech products
- People staring at computer screens (unless very subtle background element)
- Generic office spaces with overhead fluorescent lights
- Obvious stock photo aesthetics

## **Subject Matter by Article Theme**

### **Infrastructure/Foundation Articles**
**Metaphors:**
- Architectural details (beams, joints, foundations)
- Construction materials (steel, concrete, precision)
- Engineering elements (blueprints, measurements)

**Style:** Clean Industrial or Moody Cinematic
**Example:** Steel beam intersections with soft natural light, concrete foundation with dramatic shadows

---

### **Speed/Performance Articles**
**Metaphors:**
- Motion blur (vehicles, light trails)
- Kinetic energy concepts
- Flow and movement

**Style:** Motion/Energy
**Example:** Car with motion blur and light trails (like your site speed image), highway light trails at night

**Key:** Vehicle/motion metaphor works because people RELATE speed to these things - it's visceral, not literal

---

### **AI/Technology Articles**
**Metaphors:**
- Human + tech interaction (subtle, not obvious)
- Data patterns (abstract, not literal screens)
- Precision instruments
- Network connections (physical, not digital graphics)

**Style:** Moody Cinematic or Motion/Energy
**Example:** Person at desk with subtle holographic overlay (like ChatGPT ideas image), precision gauges, interconnected mechanical parts

**Key:** Tech should be **suggested** not **shown** - moody lighting with screens as ambient light sources, not as primary subject

---

### **Strategy/Systems Articles**
**Metaphors:**
- Organized tools/workspace
- Blueprint/planning elements
- Interconnected components
- Process visualization

**Style:** Clean Industrial or Moody Cinematic
**Example:** Organized drafting tools on clean surface, interlocking mechanical components, systematic arrangements

---

### **Competition/Comparison Articles**
**Metaphors:**
- Landscape juxtaposition
- Side-by-side elements
- Natural vs built environment
- Balance/measurement

**Style:** Dramatic Natural
**Example:** Desert showdown scene (like SEO Showdown), mountain peak comparison, balanced scales in nature

**Key:** Create **narrative tension** through composition - the image should suggest conflict/choice/comparison

---

### **Trust/Authority/Proof Articles**
**Metaphors:**
- Solid materials and quality craftsmanship
- Weathered but enduring structures
- Precision measurement
- Heritage meets modern

**Style:** Clean Industrial or Dramatic Natural
**Example:** Quality tool closeup, aged foundation with modern elements, precision gauge detail

---

### **Regional/Local Articles**
**Metaphors:**
- Landscape features (not touristy)
- Local architecture/structures
- Regional weather/seasons
- Community spaces

**Style:** Dramatic Natural
**Example:** Regional Australian countryside at golden hour, local streetscape, storm approaching over plains

**Key:** Authentic regional feeling - NOT postcard tourism, but **real place character**

## **Composition Guidelines**

### **Text Overlay Considerations**

**Always ensure:**
- Clean area for text (dark sky, blurred background, solid materials)
- Compositional balance (rule of thirds)
- Depth allows for text layering
- Visual interest doesn't compete with text

**Preferred Text Zones:**
- Upper third (sky, background blur, dark areas)
- Center with semi-transparent overlay option
- Lower third if subject occupies middle/top

### **Aspect Ratio & Framing**
- 16:9 for featured images (WordPress standard)
- Horizontal orientation preferred
- Leave breathing room at edges
- Consider mobile crop (center-weighted composition)

## **Brand Color Palette Integration**

**Primary Colors:**
- Ballarat Gold (#D4A574 or similar)
- Deep Blue (#1A3D5C or similar)
- Steel Gray (#6B7280)
- Clean White (#FFFFFF)

**Application:**
- Natural lighting that suggests these tones (golden hour = gold, twilight = blue)
- Material choices (brass/gold metals, steel, concrete)
- NOT artificial color overlays or heavy filters
- Subtle color grading acceptable (warmer for trust/authority, cooler for tech/precision)

**Integration Method:**
- Let materials and lighting create color palette naturally
- Avoid oversaturation
- Maintain photorealistic feel

## **Prompt Construction Formula**

### **Template:**
```
"Professional [photography style] of [conceptual subject representing article theme], 
[specific details/materials], [environment/background], [lighting description], 
[mood/atmosphere], [camera/composition notes], high-end [photography type], --ar 16:9"
```

### **Style Keywords to Rotate:**

**For Moody Cinematic:**
- "cinematic photography," "bokeh background lights," "dramatic ambient lighting," "shallow depth of field," "professional workspace photography," "evening/nighttime aesthetic"

**For Clean Industrial:**
- "architectural detail photography," "product photography," "soft natural lighting," "minimal background," "industrial precision," "high-end commercial photography"

**For Dramatic Natural:**
- "landscape photography," "golden hour lighting," "environmental portrait," "silhouette composition," "weather drama," "travel photography aesthetic"

**For Motion/Energy:**
- "motion blur photography," "light trail long exposure," "speed aesthetic," "kinetic energy," "dynamic composition," "automotive/transportation photography"

---

## **Variation Strategy**

**To maintain cohesion while avoiding repetition:**

1. **Rotate lighting styles** - Don't use same style for consecutive articles
2. **Vary conceptual approach** - Alternate between literal metaphors and abstract concepts
3. **Mix scales** - Macro details → wide landscapes → medium compositions
4. **Alternate color temperature** - Warm → cool → neutral progression
5. **Change subject categories** - Tech → nature → industrial → motion sequence

**Example Sequence for 5 Articles:**
1. Article 1: Clean Industrial (steel architecture, soft light, minimal)
2. Article 2: Moody Cinematic (workspace, bokeh, dramatic)
3. Article 3: Motion/Energy (speed blur, light trails, dynamic)
4. Article 4: Dramatic Natural (landscape, golden hour, silhouette)
5. Article 5: Clean Industrial (precision tool, product photo, detailed)

**Result:** Visually distinct images that share underlying professional aesthetic

## **Quality Checklist Before Generation**

- [ ] Style category selected based on recent article images (rotate for variety)
- [ ] Conceptual metaphor relates to article theme (but not too literal)
- [ ] Lighting creates mood appropriate to topic
- [ ] Composition allows for text overlay in at least 2 zones
- [ ] Not using overused stock photo concepts (people at computers, obvious tech)
- [ ] Maintains professional/strategic brand feeling
- [ ] Includes specific material/detail notes (not vague)
- [ ] Photography type specified (architectural/product/landscape/cinematic)

---

## **When to Break the Rules**

**Occasionally (10% of images):**
- Use unexpected subjects that create strong metaphor (car for speed)
- Go more abstract/artistic if article is conceptual
- Use humor/irony if article tone supports it
- Reference pop culture if contextually perfect (Western showdown for comparison)

**Key:** Breaking rules should be **strategic choice**, not random - the metaphor should be **immediately clear** even if unexpected.

**Example:** 
- Car speed for site performance = ✅ (universal metaphor)
- Western showdown for SEO comparison = ✅ (narrative tension)
- Random unrelated object = ❌ (confusing)

---

## **Avoid These Patterns**

**Never Generate:**
1. **Generic Tech Stock:**
   - Person typing on laptop (front view)
   - Obvious code on screen
   - Generic office with overhead lights
   - Team meetings in conference rooms

2. **Overused Digital Metaphors:**
   - Circuit boards as background
   - Binary code patterns
   - Abstract digital networks (nodes and lines)
   - Glowing tech interfaces

3. **Literal Interpretations:**
   - Website mockups as featured images
   - Google search results screenshots
   - Social media interfaces
   - Analytics dashboards (unless very subtle background)

4. **Poor Composition for Text:**
   - No clean areas for text overlay
   - Busy patterns throughout
   - Too much visual competition
   - Center-heavy with no negative space

## **Image Generation Workflow**

1. **Read article or get article theme**
2. **Check last 2-3 featured images** - identify their style categories
3. **Select different style category** for variety (rotate lighting/mood)
4. **Identify conceptual metaphor** appropriate to article theme
5. **Choose photography type** (architectural/product/cinematic/landscape/motion)
6. **Construct prompt** using formula with specific details
7. **Generate primary prompt** in DALL-E 3 or Imagen
8. **Provide alternative prompt** (different style category) as backup
9. **Include text overlay suggestions** (primary/secondary text, placement, style)

---

## **Final Reminder**

Brighter Websites imagery should feel:
- **Professional** (high-end commercial photography quality)
- **Strategic** (conceptual thinking, not literal representation)
- **Grounded** (real-world materials, authentic lighting)
- **Regional** (when applicable - not generic "anywhere")
- **Technical** (precision, infrastructure, quality visible)

**Not:**
- Generic
- Corporate
- Sterile
- Obvious
- Stock-photo-ish

**The goal:** Someone scrolling should recognize "that's a Brighter Websites article" by visual aesthetic alone, while each image feels fresh and contextually appropriate.
