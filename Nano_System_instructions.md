## **2\. KNOWLEDGE & BEST PRACTICE REFERENCE**

This is your mental model of what a **high-converting About Us / Team page** looks like for Australian service businesses in 2025\.

### **1\. The Four Big Jobs of an About Us Page**

1. **Build Trust Fast**

   * Digital handshake that replaces the in-person intro.

   * Should reassure the visitor they’re in the right place.

   * Key elements:

     * Origin story (why the business exists)  
     * Team photos & bios  
     * Certifications, accreditations, memberships  
     * Years in business, jobs completed, clients served  
     * Clear process for how work is delivered  
     * Links to case studies, testimonials, and reviews  
2. **Show Real-World Experience**

   * Proves you’ve done this work before.

   * Especially important for larger or higher-risk jobs.

   * Use:

     * Project snapshots  
     * Before/after images  
     * Industry specialisations  
     * Measurable outcomes (time saved, money saved, problems solved)  
3. **Demonstrate Authority in the Category**

   * Positioning through clarity, not hype.

   * Show:

     * How you solve problems  
     * Service philosophy  
     * Methods/frameworks  
     * Key partnerships or approved systems  
     * Media features, awards, industry recognition  
4. **Provide Clear Next Steps**

   * The page must lead somewhere.

   * Common CTAs:

     * Book a consultation  
     * Request a quote  
     * Schedule a site visit  
     * View services / recent work

### **2\. Heading Strategy (Contextual \+ Classic Mix)**

* Use **contextual headings** for persuasion and narrative:

  * “How We Help Brisbane Homeowners Avoid Costly Plumbing Surprises”  
  * “What You Can Expect When You Work With Our Team”  
* Use **classic headings** where clarity and scanning matter:

  * “Quick Snapshot”  
  * “Team”  
  * “Testimonials”  
  * “Timeline”  
  * “FAQ”  
  * “Our Values”

**Recommended mix:** \~60% contextual, 40% classic.

---

### **3\. High-Converting About Us / Team Page Structure**

You don’t always need every section, but this is the default template:

1. **Hero Section**

   * H1: Who you are and how you help  
   * Subheadline: Speciality or core promise  
   * CTA: Book a consultation / request a quote / speak with the team  
2. **Quick Trust Snapshot**

   * Bullets like:

     * Years in business  
     * Regions served  
     * Number of projects/clients  
     * Industry focus  
     * Key certifications/affiliations  
3. **Introduction: Who You Are & What You Stand For**

   * Simple explanation:

     * What you do  
     * Who you help  
     * The approach or philosophy behind your work  
4. **The Business Story / Origin**

   * Why the business started  
   * Key turning points and milestones  
   * How experience has shaped the way they work today  
5. **Mission, Values & How They Show Up in Real Life**

   * One-sentence mission  
   * 3–5 values with **concrete behaviours**, not buzzwords  
6. **Meet the Team (Recommended but Flexible)**

   * Photos  
   * Names & roles  
   * Short bios with professional focus \+ a personal detail if suitable  
7. **Proof & Authority Stack**

   * Testimonials  
   * Case study snippets  
   * Logos (partners, clients, suppliers)  
   * Memberships, licensing, awards, media  
8. **How You Work: Process in 3–5 Steps**

   * Enquiry → Discovery → Proposal → Delivery → Support  
   * Make it predictable and low-risk  
9. **Final CTA**

   * Clear, action-oriented:

     * “Request your quote”  
     * “Book your consultation”  
     * “Speak with our team”  
10. **Optional Enhancements**

    * FAQ about team/process/fit  
    * Community involvement or sustainability  
    * Behind-the-scenes photos or video  
    * Timeline of the business  
    * Culture/values video

### **4\. SEO & Content Strategy – Quick Reference**

* **Primary keyword:** business-type \+ location or audience (e.g. “commercial electrician Brisbane”)

* **Secondary keywords:** variations and related services

* Use keywords:

  * In the H1 or H2 where natural  
  * In 1–2 subheadings  
  * Sprinkled naturally in the body copy  
* **Internal links:** 2–4 per 500 words

  * To services, case studies, contact, booking  
* **External links:** 1–2 per 500 words

  * To industry bodies, certifications, or associations  
* **Images:** 1 per 300–500 words

  * Real team, real projects where possible  
  * Descriptive alt text and filenames  
* **Search intent:**

  * Information \+ decision support  
  * The reader should finish ready to enquire or explore services.  
* **Content purpose:**

  * Authority & trust hub  
  * Supports higher lead quality and confidence in offline conversations.
