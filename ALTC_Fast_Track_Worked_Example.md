# **POST-ARTICLE TASKS GUIDE**
## **ALTC Content Engine - Post-Generation Workflow**

## **Purpose**

After an article is written and before publication, these elements must be created to maximize SEO, CTR, voice search optimization, and visual appeal. This document provides specifications and requirements for each post-article deliverable.

**When to Use:** After completing article content, before publishing to website.

**Trigger Phrase:** User says **"Generate Meta and Featured Image"** (or variations)

## **DELIVERABLE 1: Voice Search TLDR**

### **Purpose**
Provide an immediate, substantive answer optimized for voice search and featured snippets. This is NOT a generic summary - it's the actual answer to the question the article addresses.

### **Requirements**

**Format:**
- 2-4 sentences maximum
- 40-60 words ideal length
- Direct answer format (not "this article will explain...")
- Conversational, natural speech patterns
- Suitable for Google Assistant/Alexa/Siri readback

**Content Criteria:**
- ✅ Provides actual answer/takeaway immediately
- ✅ Can stand alone without article context
- ✅ Uses natural language (how people speak)
- ✅ Includes specific detail/number when relevant
- ❌ Not a meta description (doesn't sell/tease)
- ❌ Not article introduction (doesn't preview)
- ❌ Not vague/generic summary

**Structure Pattern:**

**For How-To Articles:**
"[Action] by [method]. [Key step/principle]. [Expected outcome/timeframe]."

**For Comparison Articles:**
"[Option A] works best when [condition]. [Option B] suits [different condition]. Choose based on [decision factor]."

**For Strategy/Framework Articles:**
"[Framework name] focuses on [core principle]. [How it works in 1 sentence]. [Why it matters/result]."

### **Examples**

**Article:** "How to Structure Your Website So AI Systems Cite You"

**❌ Bad TLDR (too vague):**
"This article explains how to optimize your website structure for AI citations and improve search visibility."

**✅ Good TLDR:**
"AI systems cite websites with proper schema markup, entity relationships, and E-E-A-T proof architecture. Structure your content with clear expertise signals, link related topics, and demonstrate authority through case studies. Sites with this infrastructure get cited 80% more often than those without."

---

**Article:** "The Missing CRO Step: Turning Website Enquiries into Actual Sales"

**❌ Bad TLDR:**
"Learn about the connection between online enquiries and offline sales for service businesses."

**✅ Good TLDR:**
"Service business websites need CRM integration and follow-up systems to convert enquiries into sales. Most sites stop at form fills, but offline sales require structured follow-up within 24 hours, qualifying questions, and sales scripting. This missing step is why 70% of enquiries never close."

---

**Article:** "Why AI Citations Convert Better Than Google Rankings"

**❌ Bad TLDR:**
"Discover why being cited by AI systems leads to better conversion rates than traditional rankings."

**✅ Good TLDR:**
"AI citations build pre-sale credibility that traditional rankings can't match. When ChatGPT or AI Overviews cite your business as the expert source, prospects arrive already trusting you. This shortens sales cycles by 40% and attracts higher-quality leads who've done their research."

## **DELIVERABLE 2: Article Excerpt for Archive Page**

### **Purpose**
Optimize click-through from blog archive/category pages to individual articles. This is distinct from meta description - it appears on YOUR site, not search results.

### **Requirements**

**Format:**
- 1-2 sentences
- 25-35 words ideal
- Must be completely unique from meta description
- Benefit-focused (not feature-focused)

**Content Criteria:**
- ✅ Highlights immediate value/benefit reader gets
- ✅ Creates curiosity without clickbait
- ✅ Specific enough to qualify the reader
- ✅ Different angle than meta description
- ❌ Not duplicate of meta description
- ❌ Not generic "learn about X" language
- ❌ Not focused on what article contains (focus on what reader gains)

**Structure Pattern:**

**Value-First Format:**
"[Specific outcome/benefit]. [What makes this different/unique]. [Qualification of who this helps]."

**Problem-Solution Format:**
"[Common problem reader faces]. [What this article solves]. [Result they'll achieve]."

### **Examples**

**Article:** "How to Structure Your Website So AI Systems Cite You"

**Meta Description (for comparison):**
"Learn the exact schema architecture and content structure that gets your business cited by ChatGPT and AI Overviews. Proven framework with 80% AI voice share results."

**✅ Archive Excerpt (different angle):**
"Stop losing visibility to competitors in AI search results. This framework shows the technical infrastructure that made one regional business the go-to expert cited 80% of the time in 28 days."

---

**Article:** "The Missing CRO Step: Turning Website Enquiries into Actual Sales"

**Meta Description:**

**✅ Archive Excerpt (different angle):**
"Your website gets enquiries but sales aren't happening? The problem isn't traffic or conversion rates - it's what happens after the form fills. Here's the system that turns 70% more enquiries into closed deals."

**Article:** "Why AI Citations Convert Better Than Google Rankings"

## **DELIVERABLE 3: Page Meta Title and Description**

### **Purpose**
Optimize for both SEO (search engine ranking) AND CTR (click-through rate). Must match search intent perfectly while compelling clicks.

### **Requirements - Meta Title**

**Format:**
- 50-60 characters (aim for 55)
- Include primary keyword
- Include value proposition or unique angle
- Brand name optional (depends on search type)

**Structure Pattern:**

**For How-To Queries:**
"How to [Achieve Outcome]: [Unique Method/Framework]"

**For Informational Queries:**
"[Topic]: [Unique Angle] for [Target Audience]"

**For Comparison Queries:**
"[X] vs [Y]: [Decision Framework/Criteria]"

**Content Criteria:**
- ✅ Primary keyword in first 5 words when possible
- ✅ Specific outcome or unique angle
- ✅ Matches search intent (informational, commercial, navigational)
- ✅ Compelling without clickbait
- ❌ Not keyword-stuffed
- ❌ Not generic/vague
- ❌ Not misleading about content

### **Requirements - Meta Description**

**Format:**
- 150-160 characters (aim for 155)
- Include primary + 1-2 secondary keywords naturally
- Include specific benefit or unique value
- Call to action or outcome statement

**Structure Pattern:**

"[Specific outcome/benefit]. [Unique method or proof point]. [What makes this different] - [outcome or CTA]."

**Content Criteria:**
- ✅ Expands on title promise
- ✅ Includes specific numbers, proof, or unique angle
- ✅ Natural keyword inclusion (not forced)
- ✅ Compelling to target audience
- ✅ Matches search intent exactly
- ❌ Not clickbait
- ❌ Not duplicate of title
- ❌ Not vague "learn more" language

### **Examples**

**Article 1: "How to Structure Your Website So AI Systems Cite You"**

**Primary Keywords:** AI citation, AI SEO, GEO, AI Overviews optimization

**Meta Title (56 chars):**
"AI Citation SEO: Structure That Gets You Cited as Expert"

## **DELIVERABLE 4: Featured Image Prompt**

### **Purpose**
Generate professional featured image using AI (DALL-E 3 or Imagen 3) with conceptual metaphor approach that aligns with Brighter Websites brand aesthetic. Include Alt Text and Caption Suggestion.

### **Brand Visual Language**

**Style Requirements:**
- **Photography-based** (not illustrated graphics)
- **Conceptual metaphors** (physical objects representing abstract ideas)
- **Clean, professional aesthetic** (high-end commercial photography)
- **Minimal backgrounds** (white studio, subtle gradients, clean environments)
- **Dramatic but natural lighting** (side lighting, soft shadows, depth)

**Brand Colors (for reference):**
- Ballarat gold
- Deep blue
- Clean whites
- Steel grays

**Avoid:**
- Generic stock photos
- People at computers
- Obvious tech imagery (circuit boards, code screens)
- Busy compositions
- Infographic-style graphics

### **Alt Text and Captions**
**Alt Text**
- Describe what’s literally shown
- Include functional or contextual purpose (e.g. “illustrates how structured data improves AI visibility”).
- under ~125 characters so focus on clarity, not keywords.
- natural language with 1–2 relevant keywords max,
- phrased conversationally (“AI SEO trust signals”, not “SEO, AI, Google ranking, schema”).

**Captions**
- Contextual Relevance - connect directly to both the image and the surrounding paragraph.
- Added Insight (Not Description) adds meaning, evidence, or insight that reinforces content.
- Conversational Hook or Question
- carry the same humanised rhythm and brand tone
- Connect the image to a local or technical context, Bridge the visual to a conversion idea

### **Prompt Formula**

**Structure:**
```
"Professional [photography type] of [conceptual subject], [materials/details], 
[environment], [lighting], [mood], high-end commercial photography, 
[composition notes], --ar 16:9"
```

**Photography Types to Use:**
- Architectural photography (for infrastructure/foundation articles)
- Product photography (for precision/quality articles)
- Workspace photography (for process/system articles)
- Industrial photography (for technical/engineering articles)

**Conceptual Metaphors by Article Theme:**

| Article Theme | Physical Metaphor | Photography Type |
|---------------|-------------------|------------------|
| Infrastructure/Foundation | Steel beams, concrete structures, architectural details | Architectural |
| Connections/Networks | Interlocking gears, precision joints, mechanical connections | Product/Industrial |
| Systems/Process | Blueprint schematics, organized tools, workflow setups | Workspace/Product |
| Quality/Excellence | Precision instruments, measurement tools, craftsmanship details | Product |
| Growth/Progress | Construction in progress, foundation to completion sequence | Architectural |
| Trust/Authority | Solid materials, quality finishes, professional craftsmanship | Product |
| Visibility/Discovery | Clean sightlines, clear pathways, organized structures | Architectural |

## **Workflow Integration**

### **User Trigger Phrase**
"Generate Meta and Featured Image" (or variations: "Create meta content," "Generate post-article content," etc.)

### **GPT Response Sequence**

1. **Voice Search TLDR**
   - Provide 2-4 sentence substantive answer
   - Natural speech pattern
   - Includes specific detail/outcome

2. **Article Excerpt**
   - 25-35 words
   - Benefit-focused
   - Different angle from meta description

3. **Meta Title**
   - 50-60 characters
   - Keyword + value proposition
   - Search intent match

4. **Meta Description**
   - 150-160 characters
   - Expands on title
   - Includes proof/specific angle

5. **Featured Image Prompt**
   - Core concept identification
   - Visual metaphor selection
   - DALL-E 3/Imagen prompt
   - Text overlay suggestions
   - Alternative prompt option
   - Alt Text & Caption options.

### **Quality Check Before Delivery**

Before providing post-article content, verify:

- [ ] Voice Search TLDR provides actual answer (not preview)
- [ ] Archive excerpt is completely different from meta description
- [ ] Meta title includes primary keyword in first 5 words
- [ ] Meta description length is 150-160 chars
- [ ] Search intent matches perfectly across all meta content
- [ ] Featured image prompt aligns with brand aesthetic (no people at computers, no obvious tech clichés)
- [ ] Text overlay suggestions consider background composition
- [ ] Alternative image prompt provided
- [ ] Image alt and captions provided
