# **ALTC CONTENT WORKFLOW**
## **Universal 6-Step Content Generation Process**

---

## **Purpose**

This document provides the step-by-step workflow for generating ALTC content that:
1. Passes AI detection tests (6-10% target)
2. Aligns with strategic ALTC positioning
3. Maintains authentic brand voice
4. Integrates Authority Anchors
5. Drives commercial conversions

**This workflow is universal** - applies to any business using the ALTC Framework regardless of industry.

---

## **STEP 0: Apply Humanization Rules FIRST**

**BEFORE identifying strategic context, immediately internalize these non-negotiable patterns:**

### **1. Sentence Length Variation Protocol**
- 4-word sentences. Then 15-word. Then 9-word. Then 22-word.
- NEVER write 5+ consecutive sentences of similar length
- Vary starters: Questions. Statements. "And" or "But" openings. Fragments.

### **2. Natural Language Requirements**
- Use contractions reflexively (we're, you're, it's, that's, here's, can't)
- Include conversational markers sparingly (brand-specific phrases from voice guide)
- Add hedging occasionally ("In most cases," "Generally speaking," "Usually")
- Embrace imperfections (fragments, rhetorical questions, parenthetical asides, em dashes)

### **3. AI-Detection Triggers to AVOID**
- AI-favorite words: solutions, leverage, utilize, robust, synergy, paradigm shift, holistic
- Uniform paragraph lengths (all 3-4 sentences)
- Formulaic paragraph structure (topic → details → conclusion repeating)
- Mechanical transitions (Moreover, Furthermore, Additionally)
- Three-item lists in every paragraph

### **4. Paragraph Variation Patterns**
- Mix: 1-sentence paragraphs, 2-sentence bridges, 4-7 sentence analytical sections
- Think rhythm: long, short, medium, single-line, long, short
- Never uniform structure across consecutive paragraphs

**These rules apply to EVERY piece of content.**

## **STEP 1: Identify Strategic Context**

Reference: **Business-Specific ALTC Framework**

Determine:

1. **Which ALTC cluster does this belong to?**
   - Check the business's Priority ALTC Clusters (usually 2-4 clusters)
   - Identify if this is single-cluster or bridge content (connecting multiple clusters)

2. **What's the maturity target?**
   - Entry (basic explainers, FAQs)
   - Learner/Hobbyist (practical how-tos)
   - Professional (case studies, tactical processes)
   - Expert (thought leadership, original frameworks)

3. **Which Authority Anchors should be emphasized?**
   - Anchor 1: Trust & Proof (case studies, metrics, testimonials)
   - Anchor 2: Process & Education (how-it-works, behind-the-scenes)
   - Anchor 3: Comparisons & Decision Support (X vs Y, option frameworks)
   - Anchor 4: Thought Leadership (proprietary methods, industry standards)
   - Anchor 5: Local & Community Authority (regional proof, local involvement)

4. **Which service pathway does this support?**
   - Identify the money page this content leads toward
   - Confirm commercial purpose is clear

5. **Is this bridge content?**
   - Does it connect multiple clusters?
   - Which strategic connections does it demonstrate?

## **STEP 2: Maintain Voice During Writing**

Reference: **Business-Specific Brand Voice Guide**

While writing, continuously apply:

### **Sentence Rhythm Variation**
- Refer to their specific rhythm examples in voice guide
- Mix short, medium, long sentences deliberately
- Vary sentence starters throughout

### **Natural Language Patterns**
- Use their approved contractions list
- Include their conversational markers (sparingly)
- Add occasional hedging phrases
- Embrace their voice's natural imperfections

### **Industry-Specific Vocabulary**
- ALWAYS USE list (technical terms that prove expertise)
- NEVER USE list (AI-favorite words, corporate jargon)
- Replace generic terms with industry-specific language

### **Paragraph Length Variation**
- One-sentence paragraphs for emphasis
- Two-sentence paragraphs for transitions
- 4-7 line analytical paragraphs for depth
- Never uniform structure

### **Regional/Geographic Authenticity** (if applicable)
- Include location-specific references when relevant
- Use local market dynamics, seasonal patterns, community context
- Reference regional case studies naturally

## **STEP 3: Integrate Proof**

Reference: **Both ALTC Framework (for proof assets) and Brand Voice Guide (for citation style)**

### **Identify Proof Type Needed**

From ALTC Framework:
- Case study metrics (specific numbers with timeframes)
- Algorithm update data (comparative performance)
- Technical performance data (site speed, Core Web Vitals)
- Client revenue outcomes (business impact)
- Industry credentials (certifications, years of experience)
- Customer testimonials (reviews, feedback)

### **Cite Proof Naturally**

From Brand Voice Guide:

❌ **Mechanical citation:** "According to our case study, [Client] achieved [metric]"

✅ **Natural citation:** "[Client] secured [metric] in [timeframe]"

### **Connect Proof to Framework**

Don't just state what happened - explain WHY it worked:
- Link outcomes to strategic approach
- Reference Authority Anchors used
- Connect to technical implementation
- Show cause-and-effect relationship

## **STEP 4: Structure Content**

Reference: **Brand Voice Guide Content Type Templates**

### **For Blog Posts/Educational Articles:**

**Opening:**
1. Problem or data hook (what's the challenge/opportunity?)
2. Proof (immediate credibility with specific outcome)
3. Framework preview (what you'll explain)

**Body:**
1. Strategic "why" with evidence (context and reasoning)
2. Tactical "how" with process steps (implementation details)
3. Regional/industry examples (specificity and relevance)
4. Business outcomes (what results to expect)

**Closing:**
1. Strategic summary (key takeaway)
2. Service connection (natural bridge to money page)
3. Low-pressure CTA (conversational invitation)

### **For Service Pages:**

**Hero Headline:** [Outcome] for [Target Market] [Geographic Context if relevant]

**Opening:**
1. Problem (what they're facing)
2. Solution (your approach)
3. Proof (immediate credibility)

**Body:**
1. Benefits with proof
2. Process transparency
3. Pricing clarity (where appropriate)

**CTA:** Direct, low-pressure, respects intelligence

### **For Case Studies:**

**Context:**
1. Client situation (who they were, what they needed)
2. Challenges (specific problems)
3. What they came to you for

**Implementation:**
1. What you built (technical depth)
2. Strategic approach (why you made these choices)
3. Process (how it unfolded)

**Outcomes:**
1. Specific metrics with timeframes
2. Business impact (revenue, leads, efficiency)
3. Client feedback

**Why It Worked:**
1. Framework explanation (Authority Anchors used)
2. Strategic reasoning (infrastructure approach, positioning)
3. Proof of methodology (why this wasn't luck)

## **STEP 5: Quality Check**

**Before finalizing content, verify in this priority order:**

### **PRIORITY 1: Humanization (NON-NEGOTIABLE)**

Reference: **Brand Voice Guide - Humanisation Voice Requirements & Testing Protocol**

- [ ] Sentence lengths vary dramatically (4-25 words) - **count consecutive similar-length sentences**
- [ ] Contractions used naturally - **count formal constructions**
- [ ] Paragraph lengths vary (1-sentence, 2-sentence, 4-7 lines) - **never uniform**
- [ ] No AI-favorite words - **search document for: solutions, leverage, synergy, robust, utilize**
- [ ] Conversational markers included sparingly (2-3 per 1000 words)
- [ ] Hedging used occasionally ("usually," "in most cases")
- [ ] Natural imperfections present (fragments, "And"/"But" starters, rhetorical questions)
- [ ] No formulaic paragraph patterns (topic → details → conclusion repeating)

**IF HUMANIZATION FAILS, STOP AND REWRITE. Strategic accuracy means nothing if content is flagged as AI-generated.**

### **PRIORITY 2: Strategic Alignment**

Reference: **ALTC Framework**

- [ ] Content belongs to correct ALTC cluster
- [ ] Maturity level is appropriate for target audience
- [ ] Authority Anchors are properly integrated with specific proof
- [ ] Service pathway connection is clear and natural
- [ ] Commercial purpose is evident without being pushy

### **PRIORITY 3: Voice Authenticity**

Reference: **Brand Voice Guide**

- [ ] Tone matches brand characteristics
- [ ] Technical concepts explained at appropriate level
- [ ] Industry-specific vocabulary used correctly (not generic business-speak)
- [ ] Regional/local context included where relevant
- [ ] Every claim has proof backing
- [ ] CTA matches brand's style (direct, low-pressure, etc.)
- [ ] Reads like the business owner/expert speaking (not AI-generated)

### **Final AI Detection Test:**

- Run through humanization checklist one more time
- If you had to write this by hand on paper, would these sentence patterns feel natural?
- Are there 5+ consecutive sentences of similar length anywhere? (If yes, vary them)
- Did you use "leverage," "utilize," "solutions," "robust," or "synergy" anywhere? (If yes, replace)
- Are paragraph lengths varied throughout? (short, medium, long mix)

## **Critical Success Factors**

### **What Makes Content Work**

1. **Humanization executed properly** - all techniques applied simultaneously
2. **Strategic alignment clear** - cluster, maturity, anchors, service connection obvious
3. **Voice authentic** - sounds like the actual business, not generic AI
4. **Proof integrated naturally** - cited conversationally, not mechanically
5. **Commercial purpose evident** - leads somewhere specific without being pushy

### **What Causes Failure**

1. **Humanization skipped or applied selectively** - AI detection spikes
2. **Strategic confusion** - content doesn't clearly belong to cluster or connect to service
3. **Generic voice** - could be anyone in the industry saying this
4. **Proof missing or mechanical** - claims without backing or robotic citations
5. **No commercial pathway** - orphaned educational content with no conversion purpose
