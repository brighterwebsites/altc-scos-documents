# **Competitive Comparison Framework**

*Medium-Depth, Agency-Ready Version* *With Industry Modifiers \+ Full/Partial Analysis Logic*

---

# **1\. Purpose of Competitive Comparison**

To evaluate how a business’ social presence stacks up against competitors based on:

* Visual identity  
* Content quality  
* Content variety  
* Posting consistency  
* Engagement patterns  
* Trust elements  
* Brand positioning clarity  
* Conversion readiness

This comparison must be **fair** and **contextual**, considering industry norms and data visibility.

---

# **2\. Two-Path Competitive Analysis System**

GPT must always determine which path to use **before** analysing competitors.

---

## **A. FULL ANALYSIS (Path 1\)**

Use this when the following are visible for *both* the client **and** the competitor:

* Profile screenshot  
* Grid/feed (9–12+ posts)  
* Captions  
* Engagement signals (likes/comments count visible)  
* Highlights or equivalent

**If 3 or more of these elements are missing for either party → switch to PARTIAL ANALYSIS.**

---

## **B. PARTIAL ANALYSIS (Path 2\)**

Used when screenshots are limited, incomplete, or inconsistent between profiles.

In this mode GPT must:

1. Analyse only what is visible

2. Avoid judging unseen indicators (e.g., reel performance, follower count)

3. Use wording such as:

   * “Based on the available screenshots…”  
   * “Cannot compare X due to missing data…”  
4. Provide **meaningful insights** based on the cherry-picked elements that *do* appear

5. Create an “Items Not Assessable” list for transparency

---

# **3\. Competitive Comparison Criteria**

Each competitor should be compared across the same seven categories.

---

## **A. Visual Identity Comparison**

Evaluate differences in:

* Colour consistency  
* Typography  
* Image quality  
* Professionalism  
* Cohesiveness  
* Brand polish

### **What to look for**

* Does the competitor look more premium?  
* Does your client’s brand feel stronger visually?  
* Does one brand feel DIY while the other feels intentional?

---

## **B. Content Quality & Consistency**

Compare:

* Variety  
* Templates  
* Reels vs static posts  
* Production quality  
* Caption quality  
* Layout and design structure

### **What to identify**

* Competitors posting more strategic content?  
* Competitors using better templates?  
* Your client doing something unique/differentiated?

---

## **C. Posting Frequency**

Only rate what is visible.

### **Indicators**

* Competitor posts several times per week → stronger consistency  
* Large gaps between competitor posts → opportunity for your client

---

## **D. Engagement Patterns**

Screenshots often show:

* Likes per post  
* Comment counts  
* Recency of posts  
* Comment quality (spam vs genuine)

### **Interpret carefully:**

* Higher engagement ≠ better business  
* Industry norms vary  
* Screenshot-only data can’t show reach

Use language such as:

“Appears higher/lower relative to the visible screenshots.”

---

## **E. Trust Signals**

Compare:

* Reviews in posts  
* Before/after  
* Case studies  
* Social proof  
* Client photos  
* Real-world examples

### **What difference matters**

* Competitor shows abundant proof → trust advantage  
* Client has cleaner case studies → clarity advantage  
* Competitor has poor proof → your client can win here

---

## **F. Brand Positioning & Messaging**

Compare:

* Clarity of services  
* Value proposition  
* Tone of voice  
* Use of local positioning  
* Offer structure

### **Example outputs**

* “Competitor positions themselves as premium; client positions as approachable.”  
* “Client’s messaging is clearer than competitor’s.”

---

## **G. Conversion Path**

Compare:

* Bio/About section clarity  
* Highlight organisation  
* Links/Linktree optimisation  
* CTA strength  
* Website consistency  
* Service clarity

### **What matters**

* Competitor makes booking easier  
* Client has clearer CTA  
* Competitor lacks clarity (opportunity)

---

# **4\. Industry Expectation Modifiers**

GPT must **adapt comparisons** based on industry norms.

---

## **A. High-Visual Industries (wedding, beauty, design)**

Higher expectations for:

* Reels  
* Aesthetic consistency  
* High-quality imagery  
* Before/after or portfolio quality

Lower expectations for:

* Text-heavy posts  
* Technical education

---

## **B. Technical/B2B Industries (SEO, web design, law, accounting)**

Higher expectations for:

* Educational content  
* Value-based posts  
* Clarity of offer  
* Demonstrated expertise

Lower expectations for:

* Reels  
* Highly aesthetic feeds

---

## **C. Trades (plumbing, roofing, landscaping)**

Higher expectations for:

* Before/after  
* Process content  
* Local content

Lower expectations for:

* Aesthetic perfection

---

## **D. Personal Service Providers (coaches, therapists, NDIS providers)**

Higher expectations for:

* Brand personality  
* Soft educational content  
* Testimonials

Lower expectations for:

* Fast-paced reels or trends

---

GPT should reflect these expectations in comparison output.

---

# **5\. Competitive Scoring (Client vs Competitor)**

GPT must create a simple scoring comparison using this matrix:

| Category | Client | Competitor | Who Leads? |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Visual Identity | X/10 | X/10 | Client/Competitor |
| Content Quality | X/10 | X/10 | Client/Competitor |
| Posting Frequency | X/10 | X/10 | Client/Competitor |
| Engagement | X/10 | X/10 | Client/Competitor |
| Trust Signals | X/10 | X/10 | Client/Competitor |
| Messaging | X/10 | X/10 | Client/Competitor |
| Conversion Path | X/10 | X/10 | Client/Competitor |

**Totals are NOT required** — category-by-category comparison is enough.

---

# **6\. Items Not Assessable (For Partial Analysis Path)**

GPT must explicitly list items it cannot assess due to screenshot limitations.

Examples:

* Full engagement rate  
* Follower growth  
* Story activity  
* Profile traffic  
* Reach or impressions  
* Bio link functionality  
* Competitor post insights  
* Reel view numbers  
* Historical consistency

Outputs MUST include:

**“Not Assessable Based on Provided Screenshots:”**

* (list items)

---

# **7\. Example Competitive Comparison Output Snippets**

### **Example: Balanced & Fair**

“Based on the screenshots, your competitor posts more frequently and shows more before/after content. However, your visual identity appears more consistent and professional.”

---

### **Example: Screenshot-Limited Comparison**

“Only limited data was visible for the competitor, so this comparison focuses solely on visual identity and content structure.”

---

### **Example: Industry-Adjusted**

“As a wedding photographer, your competitor displays a more cohesive aesthetic, which is expected in this industry. However, your personality-led posts give you a stronger emotional connection advantage.”

---

# **8\. Recommended Actions Based on Competitive Gaps**

GPT should recommend actions like:

* Increase proof-driven content  
* Improve visual branding to match competitor quality  
* Add educational posts (especially in technical industries)  
* Add location references to match competitor reach  
* Strengthen CTA clarity  
* Introduce consistent templates  
* Add brand personality posts to differentiate
